Welcome to Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh, situated in the heart of India, is one of the most culturally rich and historically important states in the country. It is home to countless temples, forts, monuments, and spiritual destinations that attract millions of tourists every year.

Why Visit Uttar Pradesh?

  • Spiritual Hub: Home to holy cities like Varanasi, Ayodhya, Mathura, and Vrindavan.
  • Festivals: Celebrate grand festivals like Holi in Mathura-Vrindavan and Diwali in Ayodhya.
  • Natural Beauty: Scenic beauty in places like Chitrakoot and the ghats of the Ganga in Varanasi and Prayagraj.
  • Taj Mahal: World-famous symbol of love and one of the Seven Wonders of the World, located in Agra.
  • Fatehpur Sikri: Historic Mughal capital city built by Akbar, known for Buland Darwaza and UNESCO heritage structures.
  • Jhansi: Land of the brave Rani Laxmibai, with the iconic Jhansi Fort showcasing India's freedom struggle.

Famous Tourist Places

1. Taj Mahal (Agra)

  • One of the Seven Wonders of the World.
  • Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.
  • A symbol of love and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

2. Fatehpur Sikri

  • A historic city founded by Mughal Emperor Akbar.
  • Known for Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, and Panch Mahal.
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site.

3. Varanasi

  • Also known as Kashi, one of the oldest living cities in the world.
  • Famous for the holy river Ganga, ghats, and temples.
  • The Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat is world famous.

4. Vrindavan

  • Associated with the childhood of Lord Krishna.
  • Home to hundreds of temples.
  • Famous for ISKCON Temple and Prem Mandir.

5. Mathura

  • Birthplace of Lord Krishna.
  • Main attraction: Krishna Janmabhoomi temple.
  • Known for colorful Holi celebrations.

6. Ayodhya

  • Birthplace of Lord Rama.
  • Famous for the grand Ram Mandir.
  • One of the most sacred cities in Hinduism.

7. Prayagraj

  • Famous for Triveni Sangam (confluence of Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati).
  • Hosts the world’s largest religious gathering – Kumbh Mela.
  • Important pilgrimage site in Hinduism.

8. Chitrakoot

  • Lord Rama spent 11 years of exile here.
  • Known for natural beauty with hills and rivers.
  • Important pilgrimage place with several temples.

9. Jhansi

  • Historic city associated with Rani Laxmibai, a symbol of bravery.
  • Jhansi Fort is the main attraction.
  • Played a key role in India’s First War of Independence (1857).
